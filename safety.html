Dry Fire is Dangerous!

Most mishaps occur when a trigger is pulled under the false belief the firearm is not loaded! Never point your weapon at anything you are not willing to destroy.

Follow these rules religiously to prevent a mishap:

  1. Unload your firearm and remove all ammunition from the room.
  2. Choose a safe backstop and tape a target to it. Do not use something normally in the room as a target.
  3. Double check your firearm is not loaded.
  4. Dry fire practice for only five to fifteen minutes at a time. Stop practice if your concentration is weakening.
  5. When you are done put your weapon down and say out loud: "Dry Fire Practice is now over."
  6. Remove and stow your target until your next practice session.

Follow the above process every time you dry fire. This is the way.
